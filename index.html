Abstract

Because of the interferometric nature of modern gravitational telescopes they have non-uniform sensitivity. I have been able to create a tool for simple visualizations of single detector detection geometries for arbitrary binary neutron star inspirals. I generate a series of inspirals with random characteristics over a grid of sky positions. These are then used to calculate surfaces at which a certain percent of events would be detected by the chosen detector, these surfaces are known as percentile surfaces. As these surfaces are dependent on the rotation of the earth, I.E. the time of day, I have also added the ability to create surfaces that are time averaged over the course of a day.
